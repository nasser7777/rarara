Tirinyi Bee Keepers

Tirinyi Bee Keepers'. the Association was established in 2017 in Tirinyi, Kibuku District, it is a registered community-based organization. Operating as a non-political and nonprofit entity, the association is deeply rooted in environmental and livelihood initiatives. It focuses on elevating the income levels of vulnerable community members engaged in agriculture, particularly those involved in apiculture and tree planting as sustainable businesses.

WHY Tirinyi


The association employs a dynamic approach, combining enterprising activities, savings, and loans to empower its members. This strategy aligns with national development goals, including the National Development Plan (NDP), the Participatory Development Model (PDM), and the Millennium Development Goals (MDGs). Key objectives encompass achieving equity, equality, and addressing economic imbalances to foster sustainable developmen

OUR SUCCESSprogress

Tirinyi Bee Keepers proudly celebrates the current success of managing 101 bee hives, showcasing our commitment to sustainable beekeeping and community empowerment. With ambitious goals on the horizon, we aim to expand to 500 bee hives within the 3yrs, anticipating a remarkable increase in honey production. Our target of producing
10,000 liters of honey in every six-month term aligns with a broader vision to achieve an annual production plan of 20,000 liters per year. These milestones reflect not just numerical achievements but our unwavering dedication to economic empowerment, environmental sustainability, and fostering a thriving beekeeping community. We express gratitude to our supporters and community members as we collectively work towards a future buzzing with success.
